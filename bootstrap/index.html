No More Wait

Ever had that moment at a restaurant when you can't get ahold of your waiter when your glass is empty?

Ever wondered what would be a great drink to order next?

Ever wondered if you could keep track of all the drinks you ordered?

Stop wondering now.

We, the Filloaster Team, sought to solve these issues in modern society to enhance your special dining experience, whether that be at a local bar, a neighborhood cafe, or a high class restaurant.

What is it?

Filloaster is truly a "smart coaster". It has a built in scale attached to measure the amount of drink you have in your glass, and will alert a waiter to refill it when necessary. In addition, it keeps track of how much you drank, of what drink, and sends that information right back to your phone at the end of the meal. We also provide some suggestions along the lines of your next drink, or a small appetizer that may go well with a given drink. We also took this another step further, so Filloaster can be your wing man. If Filloaster thinks you're drinking too much, you probably are, and Filloaster will let you know to slow down for the night.

Coaster

The coaster portion of Filloaster works as a sensor to recognize what is happening around it. It is based on an Arduino Uno with an NFC reader and a load cell attached to it. Through the NFC reader we receive your phone number and through the load cell we receive the amount of drink.

Server

That data obtained from the coaster is transmitted to the web server. The server is written in NodeJS and implements SocketIO to achieve realtime feed back on both the customer's side as well as the restaurant's side. The server is essentially a relay to pass information given from the coaster to other aspects of the project.

User

Finally the end user can receive data from its coaster via text messages, using a popular service called Twilio. This design choice was made primarily to ease the work done on the user end, so that they don't have to install "another" app onto their phone. The integration of these three systems combine to be the final product, Filloaster.
